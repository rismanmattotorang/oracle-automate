Period-End Close in Oracle General Ledger

The period-end close in Oracle General Ledger consists of closing the subledgers, running Create Accounting and Transfer Journal Entries to GL, executing balance revaluation for foreign-currency balances, opening and closing accounting periods, and reviewing the close monitor and trial balance.

Use Manage Accounting Periods to control which accounting periods are open for each ledger; the status is held in GL_PERIOD_STATUSES.CLOSING_STATUS (O=open, C=closed, P=permanently closed, F=future).

Foreign-currency revaluation is run via the Revalue Balances scheduled process; it revalues open balances at the period-end rate and posts the unrealised gains and losses to the configured accounts.

Subledger detail in XLA_AE_LINES is rolled into GL_JE_LINES by Transfer to GL; confirm everything has transferred before closing the period.
